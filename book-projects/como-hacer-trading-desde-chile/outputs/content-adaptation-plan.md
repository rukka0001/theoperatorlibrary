# Content Adaptation Plan — Cómo Hacer Trading Desde Chile

**Source:** *El Trader Que Perdía Ganando* (ES), extracted from EPUB (34 chapters, ~17,850 words).
**Target:** *Cómo Hacer Trading Desde Chile* — same operator-led voice, restructured into two big Parts, with a new Chile-first onboarding Part and the retained risk/psychology core.

---

## Guiding principles

1. **Keep the strongest, hardest-won material** — the chapters about losses, risk, stops, position sizing, journaling, daily max loss, overtrading, revenge trading, and "winning trades while losing money." This is the book's spine and stays.
2. **Lower the on-ramp.** The current book assumes the reader already wants to short US small caps. The new book is for **someone in Chile who is curious about trading but doesn't yet know the landscape.** Part 1 is new and explanatory.
3. **Localize, don't financialize.** Chile-specific content stays **educational and descriptive** (how markets/brokers/currency/hours generally work for someone operating from Chile) — never specific buy/sell, tax, or legal advice.
4. **No promises.** Strip/avoid anything that could read as a profit promise. Keep the honest, "here is how I lost money and what I changed" tone.
5. **Self-contained.** A reader should understand each concept the first time it appears (define jargon inline: spread, leverage, VWAP, float, etc.).

### Dropped / de-emphasized from the original
- Deep **short-side strategy manual** (ch. 10–12: anatomy of a trade, "pop fallido hacia resistencia", variations) — **demoted**. The new book is broader/earlier-stage; detailed short tactics don't fit a "curious beginner in Chile" frame and risk reading as specific trade advice. Keep at most a brief, clearly-educational mention; do not lead with it.
- **"Encontrando Tu Gente"** (community) — optional short mention, not a chapter.

## Voice & readability adjustments

- Keep first-person, blunt, "I lost money, here's what changed" tone.
- Define every English/jargon term on first use (the current book mixes English trading terms freely; the new reader needs glosses).
- Shorter paragraphs and more concrete Chile-framed examples ("son las 9:30 en Nueva York — las 11:30 en Chile…").
- Every Chile-specific mechanic (money movement, currency, costs) gets a one-line **"esto es educativo, no es asesoría"** reminder where relevant.

---

## Compliance guardrails (applied throughout)

- ❌ No specific buy/sell recommendations or signals.
- ❌ No tax advice, no legal advice, no profit promises or performance guarantees.
- ✅ Educational framing; "consult a professional for your situation" where money-movement/tax/legal topics arise.
- ✅ Explicit risk-of-capital-loss disclaimer in Part 1 close, final chapter, and back matter — matching the existing product's disclaimer language.
